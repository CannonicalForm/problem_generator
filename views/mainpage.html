A Random Problem Generator

This is a Mathematics test covering reducing arithematic expressions and finding the roots to linear, quadratic, and cubic equations.

Arithematic expresssions, such as \(3 * (\frac{4}{9} + 7)\) are meant to be reduced to a single term: in this case the solution is \(\frac{67}{3}\). If manipulating fractions, or performing arithematic is uncomfortable, I would suggest visiting one of the links to the side. They provide a detailed overview of arithematic operations, and a few problem solving strategies.

Finding the roots of an algebraic equation amounts to finding the point, or points at which the equation is zero. For instance, the equation \(x^{2} - 4 = 0\) is valid for \(x = 2 \) and \(x = -2 \). Then the roots of this equation are 2 and -2. There is a thorough guide to solving polynomial equations, like the ones that will be tested on here on the sidebar.
